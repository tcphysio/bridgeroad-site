About Bridge Road Physiotherapy

Bridge Road Physiotherapy is a single-clinician practice inside Uplift Gym in Richmond. Small on purpose. You see one physio, you get their full attention, and you get a plan you understand.

The short version

I'm Thihan Chandramohan. I've worked in high-performance sport, including professional cricket, where the job is simple to state and hard to do: get people back to performing, and keep them there. The same principles apply whether you're chasing a national selection or a parkrun PB. That's the clinic in one paragraph.

Why this clinic exists

Most people who get hurt don't need a gadget or a longer course of treatment. They need an accurate read on what's wrong, a plan that builds capacity, and someone who explains it without the jargon. Plenty of clinics sell passive treatment because it fills a calendar. I'd rather you needed me less, sooner.

Being inside a gym isn't a marketing line. Real rehab needs load, space and equipment. When the fix for your knee is to get it stronger under weight, the squat rack is ten steps away, not a referral somewhere else.

How I work with you

  • I assess properly before I treat. The first session is mostly listening and testing.
  • I tell you what I think is going on, in plain terms, and what I'm less sure about.
  • We agree on a plan with a clear goal and a rough timeline.
  • Most of the work is yours to do between sessions. I make that work specific and doable.
  • We adjust as the picture changes. Rehab is rarely a straight line.

On communication

If you leave a session unsure what's wrong or what to do next, I haven't done my job. Ask me to repeat anything. I'd rather spend two minutes being clear than have you guess for a fortnight.

Part of Richmond

The clinic sits on Bridge Road, among the people it serves: locals who run the Yarra, lift at Uplift, ride to work, and want to keep doing it. That's the community this practice is built for.
