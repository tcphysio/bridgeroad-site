FAQ

Common questions

No referral is needed for a private physiotherapy appointment at Bridge Road Physiotherapy. You can book directly online or by phone. A referral is only required for specific funding arrangements, such as a Medicare Chronic Disease Management (CDM) plan from your GP.

Yes. Bridge Road Physiotherapy uses Tyro Health, so eligible private health fund rebates are claimed instantly through the EFTPOS terminal at the time of your appointment. You only pay the gap.

  • Tyro Health: most major Australian health funds are supported for on-the-spot claiming.
  • No paperwork: no separate claim form or waiting for a refund.

Medicare Chronic Disease Management (CDM) plans are accepted with a valid GP referral. WorkCover and TAC claims are not currently accepted at Bridge Road Physiotherapy.

  • Medicare CDM: bring your GP referral and plan to your first appointment.
  • WorkCover and TAC: not currently supported. Contact the clinic if you're unsure which category applies.

Yes. Telehealth video consultations are available for follow-ups, exercise programming and ongoing rehab support, wherever you are. The first appointment is best done in person at Bridge Road where possible, for an accurate hands-on assessment.

Metered and time-limited street parking is available on and around Bridge Road, Richmond, near the clinic. Spaces can fill quickly at peak times, so public transport is often the easier option.

  • Trams: routes 48 and 75 run along Bridge Road, with stops a short walk from the clinic.
  • Trains: West Richmond and East Richmond stations are both within walking distance.

Bridge Road Physiotherapy offers appointments by arrangement, including early morning and evening times, to fit around work and training schedules. Contact the clinic directly for a time outside standard online booking hours.
