Sessions

What happens when you come in

Two things people want to know before they book: what actually happens, and what it costs. Here's both, without the runaround.

Your first session

Around 45 minutes. Most of it is me understanding your problem before I touch anything.

  • We talk. What happened, what aggravates it, what you're trying to get back to. Your goal shapes the plan.
  • I assess. Movement, strength and the specific tests that point to a diagnosis.
  • I explain. What I think is going on, how confident I am, and what it means for your timeline.
  • We plan. A clear next step, usually including treatment on the day and a short set of things to do at home.

You leave with a diagnosis or a working one, a plan, and a clear idea of what comes next.

Follow-up sessions

Usually 30 minutes. This is where the actual progress happens.

  • We re-test. Compare against last time so progress is measured, not assumed.
  • We progress the plan. Load goes up, exercises change, as your capacity improves.
  • We troubleshoot. Rehab is rarely linear. We adjust when the picture changes.
  • We plan the return. As you improve, the focus shifts to getting you back to your sport or activity on clear criteria.

How treatment works here

The plan is built around movement and load, with hands-on treatment used where it speeds things up rather than as the whole approach. You'll do most of the work between sessions; I make that work specific so it actually helps. The aim is to need fewer appointments over time, not more.
